CARDIO PROTOCOL

Cardio accelerates fat loss without killing recovery. Starts conservative given your asthma history — always keep your inhaler nearby.

Weeks 1–4 · Foundation
Incline Walk
30 minutes
Treadmill at 10–12% incline, 5.5km/h

Low intensity — you should be able to hold a full conversation. Perfect for fat burning without spiking your heart rate too high.
Weeks 5–8 · Build
Walk / Jog Intervals
35 minutes
2 min walk → 1 min light jog, repeat

Moderate intensity — slightly breathless but controlled. Asthma check: if it flares, drop back to incline walks until it settles.
Weeks 9–12 · Peak
Steady Jog
40 minutes
Steady state jog OR extended intervals

Moderate-high intensity. By now your fitness will have improved significantly. Push yourself but don't go so hard that recovery suffers.

TDEE ~2,900 kcal. Protein set at 2.4g/kg (~260g/day) — shown in clinical trials to maximise muscle retention during a deficit. Calories reduce each phase as your metabolism adapts. Carbs stay around workouts. Every day has a unique meal plan.

The Other Stuff

LIFESTYLE

Training and food are 70% of the equation. The rest comes down to sleep, stress, and staying consistent when motivation dips.

😴
SLEEP
Target 7–8 hours every night. You mentioned averaging 5–6 — this needs to change. Poor sleep increases ghrelin (hunger hormone) and drops testosterone, making fat loss significantly harder.

  • Set a consistent bedtime — same time every night
  • No screens 30 mins before bed if possible
  • Magnesium Glycinate before bed will genuinely help
  • Keep your room cool and dark
🧠
STRESS
High stress = high cortisol = harder to lose fat, easier to store it. You said stress is constant and you're fine with that — but we can manage it without eliminating it.

  • Training itself is one of the best stress relief tools — use it
  • Walk during your lunch break — desk job means you need daily movement
  • 10 mins of deep breathing or a walk after work makes a difference
  • Don't skip rest days — they matter
📸
TRACK PROGRESS
